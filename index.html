Why Carry a Fire Extinguisher in Your Vehicle?

The NFPA reports that U.S. fire departments respond to an average of 171,500 vehicle fires annually. Electrical faults, fuel leaks, overheated brakes, and engine compartment failures are the leading causes. A compact, UL-rated extinguisher stored within arm's reach gives you a critical window to suppress a small fire before it spreads — or to create a safe escape path.

Key fact: Most vehicle fires become uncontrollable within 3–5 minutes. Having an extinguisher rated for Class B (flammable liquids) and Class C (electrical) fires is essential for automotive use.

Understanding Fire Extinguisher Ratings

Class B — Flammable Liquids

Covers gasoline, diesel, oil, and other petroleum-based fires. This is the most critical rating for vehicle use.

Class C — Electrical Equipment

Covers energized electrical fires — wiring harnesses, batteries, alternators. Important for modern vehicles with complex electronics.

Class A — Ordinary Combustibles

Covers wood, cloth, paper, and plastics. Relevant for interior fires in RVs and campers with living spaces.

For most passenger vehicles, a B:C rated extinguisher is the minimum. For RVs and vehicles with living quarters, an A:B:C rated extinguisher provides broader coverage.
